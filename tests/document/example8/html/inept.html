1. INEPT and Signal Enhancement

Justin Lorieau{}

The purpose of the INEPT sequenceMorris1979 is to transfer the large magnetization from high-γ nuclei, like 1H or 19F, to low-γ nuclei (labeled ‘X’), like 13C and 15N. This transfer significantly improves the signal intensity of ‘X’ nuclei when measuring their chemical shifts. For example, the magnetic moment and magnetization of 1H nuclei is roughly 4 times greater than 13C and 10 times greater than 15N. Additionally, the 1H is typically much shorter than the for 13C or 15N, which allows a shorter recycle delay and the collection of a greater number of spectra.

1.1. Theory

The INEPT sequence (Fig. 1.1) transfers magnetization from 1H nuclei bonded the ‘X’ nuclei. The two nuclei must be bonded, or at least connected through intermediary bonds with other atoms, because the nuclei must share a J-coupling.

1.1.1. Methine, Amide and the AX Spin Systems

The first pulse creates 1H magnetization. A pulse rotates the magnetization by with a phase of ‘x’. Thereafter, the 1H pulse in the middle of the two Δ delays acts as a Hahn-echo to refocus the 1H chemical shifts. Without the accompanying pulse on the ‘X’ channel, the J-coupling between the 1H and X nucleus would be refocused as well—and nothing would be accomplished. The pulse on the ‘X’ channel acts to cancel the refocusing effect of the 1H pulse for the J-coupling.

During the first delta period, the magnetization evolves under the 1H chemical shift () and the 1H-X J-coupling. Since the chemical shift and J-coupling Hamiltonians commute, the rotations of each can be conducted sequentially.
